Chapter Two

Simeon and the First Piercing

Waiting does not leave a person neutral. Over time, it presses the soul into one of two shapes. It either sharpens discernment or it corrodes hope into bitterness. Simeon chose discernment, but that choice was not made in a moment. It was made across years, through repetition, disappointment, and silence.

Scripture gives few details about Simeon’s daily life, yet the absence of detail is itself revealing. He waited long enough that his waiting became part of who he was. He did not rush God with timelines or attempt to manufacture fulfillment. He learned how to live without answers while remaining open to recognition.

Yet Simeon knew.

His spirit recognized what sight alone could never confirm. Years of waiting had refined his perception. He had learned how to distinguish noise from presence, activity from arrival.

Salvation had entered history.

God is not divided. Salvation is not a separate work He performs from a distance. Salvation is God revealed, God present, God embodied. One God. One Savior. His name is Jesus.

Then Simeon turned toward Mary.

He did not speak fear into her future. He spoke honesty into it. He prepared her for cost.

A sword would pierce her soul.

Mary would not merely observe suffering. She would carry it within herself.

This is where faith either matures or retreats.

The first piercing did not draw blood. It drew awareness.

Mary now knew something she could not unknow. Obedience would hurt. Love would wound. Faith would cost more than celebration ever could.

And still, she stayed.
